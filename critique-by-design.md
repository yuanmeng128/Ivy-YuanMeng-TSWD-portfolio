# Assignment 3 & 4: Critique by Design

## Step 1: find a data visualization

I have a lovely pet dog who has already been 11 years old and I am going to adopt a pet dog after I graduate. I am interested in the pet adoption in America, so I am searching about the pet adoption statistics of America. During my research, I found a great website (https://spots.com/pet-adoption-statistics/#alabama) including useful statistics about the pet adoption in the U.S. and noticed a visualization in the website, which is shown below:

## Step 2 critique the data visualization

I use Stephen Few's Data Visualization Effective Profile to critique the data visualization above. Below is the detailed analysis:

- Usefulness

<img width="609" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 47 25" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217400473-0be2887c-1340-4713-8c96-9565096c910d.png">

I choose 8 for the usefulness. Since this visualization is included in a pet adoption website, the intended audience should be people interested in the pet adoption. In general, this visualization presents the pet adoption trend in recent 5 years in America clearly, which is useful for the target audience. But the information delivered by this bar chart may be not sufficient, so I grade 8 for the usefulness dimension.

- Completeness

<img width="609" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 47 36" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217401463-1f9a32a4-125e-4de1-8a00-4effb4c424b6.png">

I choose 6 for the completeness. The bar chart includes most necessary information, what's the value is, the time line, and the data of different categories. But it missed an important information that the data is of the whole America. Though audience can know that the data is about the America through the context, audience cannot know this data is of the whole America instead of a state if the chart didn't include the information. So, I just give 6 for the completeness.

- Perceptibility

<img width="609" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 47 44" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217402345-bf990297-6eef-4c6a-98f5-52e441146c57.png">

In general, the visualization deliver the information clearly. Readers can understand the information easily. However, since the chart aims to show the trend of the pet adoption, line chart may be better to use. But the bar chart can also show the trend. So, I give 7 for "perceptibility".

- Truthfulness

<img width="604" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 47 59" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217402970-0f6d0efe-4da3-4f76-a43b-d1096f407555.png">

Firstly, the chart is based on reliable data sources. Besides, it also represent the data in a complete and objective manner. The only shortage of the chart is that it missed the data range, the whole America. In general, it is accurate, reliable and valid. So, I choose 8 for the truthfulness.

- Intuitiveness

<img width="607" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 48 14" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217403661-1a8b8fae-bfc5-4017-80a4-738ed499a617.png">

In general, the chart shows the data clearly. But when we look at the chart more carefully, we can find that the value of y-axis is the change percentage of pet adoption, not percentage of pet adoption. These two concepts may get confused if audience don't look at the title carefully. When I first looked at it, I got confused. So, adding a note for the y-axis may be helpful. So, I give 6 for the intuitiveness of the chart.

- Aesthetics

<img width="605" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 48 30" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217404904-6e5b3a38-f84b-46e1-9ac3-d941c2aaff18.png">

Though the chart presents the data clearly, it didn't do a good job in the perspective of aesthetics. The color selection is a little confusing. The cat and dog categories should contrast, but the chart used the same color. The legend is at the bottom, which needs a long eye travel to match with the bar color. The percentage labeled on the bar is vertical which is also hard to look at. So, I just give 4 for the aesthetics.

- Engagement

<img width="608" alt="Screen Shot 2023-02-07 at 19 48 44" src="https://user-images.githubusercontent.com/122709931/217405702-63c02da5-8147-434d-9cb3-4d16a865f89e.png">

In general, the bar chart clearly shows the change percentage of the pet adoption from 2017 to 2021 and the changing trend in these 5 years. From the visualization, audience can find an obviously decreasing pattern of the pet adoption in America, which highly probably triggers audience to explore deeper on the reason of the decreasing pattern. So, I give 8 points for the engagement evaluation dimension. 

- Overall evaluation

In general, the chart is concise and clear. It almost has no redundant elements and presents the data clearly. This visualization did a good job in many ways. Firstly, the chart labels the percentage on the bar directly, which presents the data more concisely and greatly reduce eye travel. Secondly, the chart clearly shows the positive and negative of the change percentage by the direction of the bar. Thirdly, the title of the chart clearly informs audience what the data refers to. Fourthly, the chart gets rid of grid lines, which make the whole chart very clear. 

However, the visualization also has several flaws. Firstly, the title of the bar chart is essentially the label of the y-axis. So, the bar chart actually lacks a title that shows the core purpose of the chart. Secondly, the legend is placed at the bottom of the chart, which results in more eye travel when checking what the color refers to. Thirdly, because bars in a series are separated by bars in other series, the trend of bars in a series is not clear enough for adjacent years with close values. Fourthly, the value labeled on the bar is vertical, which is difficult to read. Fifthly, the color selection of the bar is bad. The colors for the “cat” series and “dog” series are of the same color, but the “cat” and “dog” should contrast. The “total” bars are red, contrast to “cat” and “dog” bars, which doesn’t make sense. Sixthly, the chart is displaying the data of the whole America, but the chart fails to present the information. 

In summary, though the visualization displays the data clearly, it may be just because the size of the dataset is small. There are still many improvements can be acheived for the visualization.
